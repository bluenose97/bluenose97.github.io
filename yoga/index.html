Atomic Habits System

The goal is to make the desired action obvious, easy, attractive, and satisfying.

1

Make it obvious

Implementation intention: on Sun/Wed/Fri at 7:30 AM, after bathroom/teeth and before opening messages, start the yoga video. On other days, start the 15-minute meditation timer at 7:30 AM and 9:30 PM.

2

Make it easy

Mat stays visible the night before. Phone opens directly to this plan or the video. Minimum version: 2 minutes on the mat or 3 mindful breaths. That keeps the chain alive.

3

Make it attractive

Pair the start with something you like: coffee ready, good lighting, same playlist before practice, or a post-yoga shower. The cue should feel like entering a small ritual, not obeying homework.

4

Make it satisfying

Track only one mark per day: Y for yoga, M for meditation, or MIN for minimum version. Never miss twice. A weak day still counts if you touch the habit.

Back-Aware Decision Rules

This keeps the plan reliable without ignoring useful body feedback.

  • GGreen: 0-2/10 discomfort, no sharpness, no next-day worsening. Keep the video.
  • YYellow: around 3/10, lingering discomfort, or guarded movement. Modify more next time.
  • RRed: sharp pain, catching, leg symptoms, numbness, tingling, weakness, or flare. Stop that pose or video.

Bias toward

  • Cat/cow style mobility
  • Low cobra or sphinx instead of aggressive backbends
  • Hip mobility, glute engagement, and core control
  • Bent-knee modifications in folds and hamstring work

Avoid for now

  • Deep backbend, heart-opener, or backbend-intensive classes
  • Forcing twists, folds, or lunges to end range
  • Using Friday yoga as toughness training before soccer
  • Changing all three videos at once
