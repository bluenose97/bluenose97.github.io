Flexible Tasmania Campervan Itinerary

A balanced 5-night loop from Hobart with mostly free camps, a couple of worthwhile low-cost sites, near-van quiet saxophone spots, winter-friendly hikes, whale lookouts, pie stops, and a calmer MONA finish.

Dates Sun 26 Jul - Fri 31 Jul Van 1 PM pickup / 1 PM drop-off Flight Fri 8:00 PM from Hobart Style Balanced, flexible, low-stress
Day 1 Day 2 Day 3 Day 4 Day 5 Day 6 Bruny Swap

Day 1: Sunday 26 July - Hobart Airport to Mayfield Bay

Drive: 2-2.5 hrs total Camp: free Toilets Beach sax spot
N1

Land at 12:05 PM, collect the van around 1 PM, then do the unglamorous setup: water tank, groceries, gas, toilet paper, trowel, and ideally a 10L backup water cube.

Afternoon Drive

Van pickup area to Richmond: 25-35 min. Richmond to Mayfield Bay: 1 hr 30 min.

Pie Stop

Richmond Bakery for a curried scallop pie. If you are running late, save the bakery idea for another day and go straight to camp.

Camp

Mayfield Bay Conservation Area. Free, toilets, beach access. Bring drinking water. Isolation is medium-high in winter, but the site is close to the road.

Quiet Sax Spot

Walk down Mayfield Beach away from other campers before sunset. Easy, close to the van, and a good first-night sound test.

Flexible options
Clear weather Add Spiky Bridge or Orford foreshore before camp.
Rain / wind Prioritise groceries, water, and an early camp setup. Play sax from a sheltered beach edge only if nobody is nearby.
Low energy Skip Richmond and eat something easy near Sorell. First day is for getting the van dialled.

Day 2: Monday 27 July - Mayfield Bay to Freycinet

Drive: 1-1.5 hrs Camp: low-cost Water + toilets Whale lookout
N2

This is the first “worth paying a little” night. Freycinet’s campground is not the loneliest camp in Tasmania, but for your water comfort, winter hiking access, and a near-van beach base, it earns its place.

Morning Drive

Mayfield Bay to Freycinet / Wineglass Bay car park: 45-55 min.

Walk + Whale Scan

Wineglass Bay Lookout, about 1.5-2 hrs return. Then Cape Tourville Lighthouse for a short walk and whale scan.

Camp

Richardsons Beach / Ranger Creek, Freycinet National Park. Paid but under $30 for a campervan-style unpowered site. Toilets, potable water, cold showers, BBQs, and immediate walk access.

Quiet Sax Spot

Richardsons Beach or Honeymoon Bay edges at dusk, away from tents and the visitor centre. Close to the van, not totally isolated, but workable in winter.

Flexible options
Clear weather Extend from the lookout down to Wineglass Bay beach if you feel good and daylight is generous.
Rain / wind Keep it to Cape Tourville and short Freycinet lookouts. Cold rain on the Wineglass steps is not character-building in a useful way.
Low energy Skip Wineglass and do Sleepy Bay / Honeymoon Bay short walks, then make the campsite the event.
More isolated free alternative

Lagoons Beach / Chain of Lagoons is the wilder free version: drop toilets and beach solitude, but you trade away potable water and Freycinet convenience. Choose it if you care more about isolation than comfort that night.

Day 3: Tuesday 28 July - Freycinet to Bicheno to Lake Leake

Drive: 2.5-3 hrs Camp: free Lake quiet Bakery + seafood
N3

Use this as the coast-to-lake reset. You get Bicheno food, another ocean scan if you want it, then a quiet inland camp where playing near the van makes sense.

Morning Drive

Freycinet to Bicheno: 35-40 min. Bicheno to St Marys / Elephant Pass: 40-50 min. St Marys to Lake Leake: 1 hr 30 min.

Food

Blue Edge Bakery for a scallop pie or steak pie. Lobster Shack Tasmania if you want fresh local seafood without making it a fancy restaurant night.

Camp

Lake Leake Campground. Free/council campground for self-contained vans, with toilets and BBQ facilities. Bring drinking water; the lake is a filterable backup.

Quiet Sax Spot

Park away from the boat ramp and anglers. This is one of the best near-van playing locations on the route: cold lake air, space, and very little fuss.

Flexible options
Clear weather Add a slow Bicheno coast walk or blowhole scan before turning inland.
Rain / wind Make it a bakery, seafood, and warm-van day. Avoid committing to exposed walks.
Low energy Go straight from Freycinet to Lake Leake via Bicheno supplies and have a long quiet camp afternoon.

Day 4: Wednesday 29 July - Lake Leake to Ross to Hartz / Arve River

Drive: 3.5-4 hrs Camp: free Forest solitude Alpine weather call
N4

This is the biggest driving day, but it buys you the forest-and-mountain night. Keep Hartz weather-led; in winter, the mountain gets to vote.

Morning Drive

Lake Leake to Ross: 45-55 min. Ross to Geeveston / Arve River: about 2.5 hrs.

Pie Stop

Convict Coffee Cafe & Bakery in Ross, formerly Bakery 31, for the scallop-pie pilgrimage. Ross Village Bakery is the second-bakery backup.

Walk

Hartz Mountains National Park: Arve Falls plus Lake Osborne is the sensible winter default, roughly 1.5-2.5 hrs total.

Camp

Arve River Camping and Picnic Area. Free-style forest camp with toilets, BBQs/tables, and river nearby. This is probably your best quiet near-van sax night.

Flexible options
Clear weather Consider Hartz Peak only if visibility, wind, road, and ice conditions are all friendly.
Rain / wind Skip alpine exposure. Do Arve River forest, Geeveston, and a sheltered camp afternoon.
Low energy Treat Ross as the outing, then get to Arve River early and play from camp before dark.

Day 5: Thursday 30 July - Arve River to Tasman Peninsula / Fortescue Bay

Drive: 2.5-3 hrs Camp: low-cost Hot showers Cliffs + whales
N5

This replaces Bruny as the recommended final night. You still get a dramatic coast, a proper wildlife/whale feel, and a special campsite, but without ferry stress before van return.

Morning Drive

Arve River to Dunalley: 1 hr 45 min-2 hrs. Dunalley to Tasman Arch / Fortescue Bay area: 45-60 min.

Pie Stop

Dunalley Bakery for a mainland-side pie stop before the Tasman Peninsula.

Lookouts / Walk

Easy stops: Tasman Arch, Devils Kitchen, Blowhole area. Bigger option: Cape Hauy, about 4 hrs return, with many steps and massive sea cliffs.

Camp

Fortescue Bay Campground, ideally Mill Creek for campervan shelter. Paid but excellent value: toilets, drinking water, hot showers, and direct access to Cape Hauy.

Quiet Sax Spot

Mill Creek forest edge if nobody is close, or the beach edge early evening. Still near the van, but it feels much more remote than Hobart-adjacent camping.

Flexible options
Clear weather Do Cape Hauy. It is the big scenic payoff of the final wild day.
Rain / wind Stick to Tasman Arch, Devils Kitchen, Remarkable Cave, and a warm Fortescue camp. Avoid exposed cliff walks.
Low energy Skip Cape Hauy and make Fortescue Bay itself the destination. Hot shower, beach, sax, early packing.
If Fortescue is full or the road feels annoying

Lime Bay Campground is the calmer low-cost backup: beachside, toilets, tank water that needs treatment, and a quieter near-van feel. It is less iconic than Fortescue, but very compatible with your sax/camp style.

Day 6: Friday 31 July - Van Return, MONA, Hobart Airport

Van return: 1 PM MONA: relaxed half-day Flight: 8 PM
Fri

Keep this day clean. You are not doing Bruny in the recommended route, so there is no final-morning ferry queue.

Morning Drive

Leave Fortescue Bay around 8:30 AM. Drive to the Hobart / airport-side van drop-off area: 1 hr 45 min-2 hrs, depending on the exact Camplify address.

MONA

Return the van by 1 PM, then aim for MONA around 2-5 PM. It is enough time for the main experience without feeling like a frantic airport layover.

Airport

Leave the MONA / Hobart area around 5:30-6 PM for Hobart Airport. Flight departs 8 PM.

Flexible options
Clear weather Add one very short Tasman Peninsula lookout at sunrise, then leave on time.
Rain / wind Direct return. Let MONA be the weatherproof highlight.
Low energy Earlier van return, longer coffee/lunch buffer, then MONA.

Optional Bruny Island Swap

Not default Ferry timing Good if you want oysters + island roads

I would only swap Bruny in if you wake up genuinely wanting island produce and ferry novelty more than Tasman Peninsula cliffs. Without the paid cruise, Bruny is lovely but less efficient for this trip.

How to swap it: on Day 5, drive Arve River to Kettering, ferry to Roberts Point, do Get Shucked / Bruny Island Cheese / Adventure Bay or Fluted Cape, then camp at The Neck or The Pines. On Friday, leave early and aim for the 8:30 or 8:50 AM ferry from Roberts Point back to Kettering.

Friday ferry times from Bruny to Kettering

Winter 2026 Roberts Point departures include: 6:30 AM weekdays only, then 7:10, 7:50, 8:30, 8:50, 9:10, 9:30, 9:50, 10:10, 10:30, 10:50, 11:10, 11:30, 12:10, 12:50, 1:30, 2:10, 2:50, 3:10, 3:30, 4:00, 4:20, 4:40, 5:00, 5:20, 6:00, 6:40, 7:15 PM. It is a queueing system, not a fixed booked departure.
